# Sharp Picks X/Twitter Content Strategy

## The Rule

Never tweet picks, edges, signals, or anything a subscriber pays for. The X account sells the *process* and the *philosophy* - not the product's output. If someone could reconstruct tonight's signals from your tweets, you're giving away too much.

---

## What TO Tweet

### 1. Process Transparency (the backbone - 40% of posts)
Show how the system thinks without showing what it picked.

### 2. Market Observations (general, non-actionable - 20%)
Comment on the market itself - line movement patterns, public betting trends, general structure. Never tied to a specific pick.

### 3. Journal Article Teasers (when articles publish - 15%)
Drive traffic to the journal. One teaser on publish day, one repost 2 days later.

### 4. Results Accountability (after games settle - 15%)
Post the record. Wins AND losses. Never hide a bad night. This is the anti-tout differentiator.

### 5. Philosophy / Hot Takes (brand voice - 10%)
Short, sharp opinions on betting culture, touts, parlays, the industry. These are the ones that get engagement.

---

## What NEVER to Tweet

- Specific picks or team names before games
- Edge percentages on specific games
- Signal counts before tip-off ("3 signals tonight" tells people too much)
- Anything that says "we like [team]" or "we're on [team]"
- Screenshots of the app showing today's active signals
- Line movement on games where you have a signal (implies the pick)

---

## Safe Zone vs. Danger Zone Examples

SAFE: "Model passed on the full slate tonight. Zero signals. The filter works."
DANGER: "Only one signal tonight - big edge on the late game." (narrows it down)

SAFE: "5-3 on the week. CLV positive across all 8 picks."
DANGER: "Hit on the Lakers last night, nice cover." (reveals the pick retroactively is fine AFTER the game, but not before)

SAFE: "The spread market is running wide tonight - lots of double-digit lines."
DANGER: "Seeing value on the underdog side of a big spread tonight." (too specific)

SAFE: "Interesting that the public is hammering favorites tonight across the board."
DANGER: "Public is wrong on the BOS/MIL line." (reveals positioning)

## Tweet Bank: Market Observations

These comment on the market without revealing positioning. Always general, never tied to a specific game.

"Spreads are running wide tonight - four games with double-digit lines. The market struggles most with lopsided matchups."

"The public is heavy on favorites tonight. That doesn't mean the favorites are wrong - it means the price might be."

"Line movement has been minimal today. When lines don't move, it usually means sharp money and public money agree. Harder to find edge in consensus."

"Big movement on a few totals today. When totals move more than spreads, it's usually injury news or pace-related information hitting the market."

"Rest day mismatches across the board tonight. The market prices rest in, but the question is always whether it prices it in correctly."

"Late scratch changing the line by 2 points. This is why we refresh lines three times a day - the morning number isn't the evening number."

"Early season vs. late season markets are completely different. Books have better data now, lines are tighter, and edges are harder to find. That's expected."

Post these the morning after games settle. Always include the full record. Never hide losses.

"Last night: [W-L on the night]. Season: [W-L overall]. CLV: [+/-]. Process is process - good nights and bad nights both get posted."

"Went 0-2 last night. It happens. The model ran the same process it always runs. Two picks, two losses. Season record: [X-Y]. Moving on."

"3-0 last night. Don't get excited - three picks is three picks. The season record moves to [X-Y]. CLV still positive. Process over results."

"Rough week. [X-Y] over the last 7 days. But CLV is still positive, which means the model is finding value the market eventually agrees with. That's the leading indicator."

"End of week recap: [X-Y]. [Z] games passed on. [A] total edges detected. The filter cut [B] of them. Record matters less than whether the process is sound."

"Monthly update: [full month record]. CLV: [+/-]. Biggest edge hit: [X%]. Biggest edge miss: [Y%]. Full transparency, always."

### After Bad Streaks

"4 losses in a row. The math says this happens. A 54% edge over 200 picks guarantees multiple 4-5 game losing streaks. The question isn't if - it's whether you change your process when it does. We don't."

"Bad stretch. Posting the record anyway because that's the deal. Season: [X-Y]. The model doesn't know it's losing. It just runs the next slate."

### After Good Streaks

"6 straight wins. Don't let it go to your head. This is variance working in our favor the same way it sometimes works against us. Same process, same stakes, same discipline."

"Hot streak disclaimer: this will end. Winning streaks feel like skill. They're mostly math. The process doesn't change when we're winning."

| Wed | Market observation OR results recap | Mid-week, general market color |
| Thu | Philosophy / hot take | Engagement driver before the weekend |
| Fri | Journal article teaser | When articles publish (Fri schedule) |
| Sat | Results accountability (weekly recap) | Morning, after Friday games settle |
| Sun | Off or light engagement (reply only) | Model retrains on Sunday - could tweet about that |

Target: 5-7 posts per week. Never more than 2 per day. Quality over volume - same philosophy as the product.

---

## Engagement Rules

1. **Reply to genuine questions.** If someone asks how the model works, give them a real answer. Link to the journal article if relevant.
2. **Don't argue with trolls.** If someone calls you a scammer, the record is public. Link to the Results tab and move on.
3. **Never dunk on other products by name.** Punch up at the industry and tout culture in general, never at specific competitors.
4. **Retweet users who share their experience** (with permission). Social proof from real users beats any marketing copy.
5. **Don't tweet during games.** It creates the temptation to comment on specific outcomes tied to picks. Post results the next morning.
6. **Never delete a tweet.** Especially bad predictions or premature celebrations. Transparency means the archive is honest.
