## THE FOUR LAYERS

Most sports betting apps do one thing: give you picks.
SharpPicks gives you four layers of intelligence that work together.

### 1. MARKET INTELLIGENCE
**What the market looks like today - before you bet anything.**

- **MEI (Market Edge Index):** A single number that quantifies today's overall market opportunity. Not every day is a betting day. MEI tells you before you look at a single game.
- **Regime Classification:** Is the market normal, volatile, or compressed? This changes how aggressively the model acts.
- **Market Signal:** A plain-language narrative explaining what the model isolated and why. When the model picks one game from a full slate, it usually means one matchup is genuinely mispriced while the rest of the market is tight.
- **Line Movement Tracker:** Which lines moved toward model, away from model, or stayed flat. Two of five lines moving toward model position tells you the market is catching up.
- **Model vs Market Delta:** Visual bars showing the gap between model projection and market line for every game. The bigger the bar, the bigger the disagreement.

*No other sports app gives you a daily market briefing before you see a single pick.*

### 2. QUANTITATIVE EDGE DETECTION
**Every signal comes with full model transparency.**

- **Four-Model Ensemble:** GBM, Random Forest, XGBoost, AdaBoost - all must agree before a signal fires. Consensus, not conviction from a single model.
- **56 Features:** The models analyze 56 quantifiable features per game. No gut feel. No narratives. Data.
- **Adjusted Edge:** The final edge after shrinkage blend (30/70 model-to-market), accounting for the fact that the market is usually right.
- **Cover Probability:** Not just "we like this team" - the actual model-estimated probability of covering, shown against the implied market probability.
- **Value Range:** "Playable down to -12 - edge invalidates beyond." This tells you the exact line where the bet stops being worth it. If the line moves past your corridor, the model says walk away.
- **Quant Reasoning:** Plain-language explanation of the matchup math - PPG differentials, rest advantages, spread-adjusted scoring margins. The model shows its work.
- **3.5% Minimum Edge Threshold:** If the model doesn't find at least 3.5% edge, nothing gets sent. Silence is a signal.

*No other sports app shows you where the edge invalidates or gives you a playable value range.*

### 3. BEHAVIORAL EDGE
**The feature nobody else has: it measures the bettor, not just the bet.**

- **Discipline Score:** A letter grade (A through F) based on how selectively you follow signals. The industry average selectivity is 78% - meaning most bettors bet on almost everything. SharpPicks users who score well are closer to 50%.
- **Selectivity Rate:** What percentage of model signals you actually bet on. Lower is usually better. Fewer decisions, better decisions.
- **Capital Preserved:** The dollar amount your bankroll saved by passing on picks you didn't follow. "Estimated bankroll saved by passing on 13 picks you didn't follow this season." Money saved compounds just like wins do.
- **Selectivity Spectrum:** A visual scale from "Sharp (Selective)" to "Square (Volume)" showing where you fall relative to the industry.
- **Bankroll Tracking:** Actual P&L curve with real stakes, ROI, unit tracking, and record - not hypothetical flat-bet projections.
- **Model vs Off-Model:** Compare your results when following the model vs going off-script. The data usually speaks for itself.

*No other sports app tells you the value of the bets you didn't make.*

### 4. SHARP JOURNAL
**The editorial layer that builds conviction and educates.**

- **Founder Letters:** Written by Evan Cole (founder persona), covering model philosophy, market mechanics, and the discipline required to win long-term.
- **Market Notes:** Same-day analysis explaining what the model saw, why certain lines moved, and what the signals mean in context.
- **Philosophy & Discipline:** Content designed to make users better bettors - not by giving them more picks, but by helping them understand when not to bet.

*No other sports app has an editorial layer that actively discourages unnecessary betting.*
