CLV Calculator

Measure Your Closing Line Value

Closing Line Value (CLV) measures whether you got a better price than the market's final number. Consistently beating the closing line is the strongest predictor of long-term betting profitability.

Closing Line Value
How CLV Works

If you bet a spread of -3 and the line closes at -4.5, you captured 1.5 points of CLV. That means you got a better number than the final market price. Over time, positive CLV correlates strongly with profit.

SharpPicks measures CLV on every signal to validate model accuracy. A high CLV+ rate confirms the model is identifying real mispricing, not noise.

Common Questions
What is Closing Line Value?
CLV measures whether you got a better price than the closing line. Consistently positive CLV indicates long-term edge.
How do I calculate CLV?
Compare the line you bet at versus the closing line. If you bet -3 and it closed at -4.5, you captured 1.5 points of CLV.
Why does CLV matter?
CLV is the strongest predictor of long-term betting profitability. Bettors who consistently beat the closing line tend to profit over time.
