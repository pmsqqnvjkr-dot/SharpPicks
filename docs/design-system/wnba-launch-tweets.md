## Tweet 3 — First Market Note (Sunday May 12, 11:00 AM ET)

**Type:** Market Note (educational, evergreen-ish)
**Goal:** Establish authority. This is the tweet that gets bookmarked and shared.

```
Three things the WNBA market gets wrong every season opener:

1. It overweights last year's playoff performance. Roster turnover and offseason additions reset the math.

2. It anchors totals to the prior season's pace. New coaching hires shift pace meaningfully.

3. It underprices rest disadvantage in early-season back-to-backs.

The first two weeks are where edges live. The market catches up by Memorial Day.
```

**Strategic notes:**
- This is the tweet that signals "we know what we're talking about." Specific, contrarian, educational
- The three-point structure is highly readable and shareable
- "The market catches up by Memorial Day" is the kind of line a sharp would underline — concrete, falsifiable, and shows you've thought about market efficiency timing
- No call to action. The whole tweet IS the brand pitch
- Numbered list works on X. Bullets don't render well

**Format template for ongoing Market Notes:**
- Lead with a contrarian observation
- 3 numbered points with specific, falsifiable claims
- Close with a one-line synthesis that frames the market timing
