You’re asking what separates elite, durable, institutional-level betting models from sharp hobby projects.

Let’s build the checklist the right way.

⸻

🧠 1. Closing Line Value Tracking (Non-Negotiable)

If you don’t measure CLV, you don’t know if you have edge.

Your system should track:
	•	Pick line
	•	Closing line
	•	CLV in points
	•	CLV in implied probability

Over 500+ bets, positive CLV = real edge.
Negative CLV but good record = variance.

This is your lie detector.

⸻

📉 2. Volatility & Drawdown Modeling

Most models optimize win rate.
Elite systems optimize survival.

Track:
	•	Max drawdown
	•	Rolling 50-bet ROI
	•	Standard deviation of returns
	•	Risk-of-ruin at fixed unit sizing

You want:
	•	Lower variance per edge point
	•	Stability across market regimes

You’re building a fund, not a hot streak.

⸻

📊 3. Market Regime Detection

The NBA changes during the season.

Early season:
	•	Underpriced team shifts
	•	Rotation chaos

Pre-All Star:
	•	Fatigue patterns

Post-All Star:
	•	Tanking signals
	•	Playoff urgency

Late season:
	•	Motivation asymmetry

Elite models have:
	•	Regime flags
	•	Or season-phase weighting

Right now you’re static.
That’s exploitable.

⸻

🧩 4. Motivation & Incentive Layer

This is uncomfortable but real.

Teams late season:
	•	Tanking
	•	Seeding jockeying
	•	Resting stars
	•	Travel compression

You need signals for:
	•	Playoff probability proximity
	•	Back-to-back before marquee matchup
	•	Long road trip final game

Markets are decent at this.
They’re not perfect.

⸻

📈 5. Line Movement Decomposition

You already penalize line movement.

Next step:
	•	Distinguish sharp steam from public steam.

Sharp steam:
	•	Early
	•	Low-volume book
	•	No injury news

Public steam:
	•	Late
	•	Primetime
	•	Big market team

Different signals.
Different meanings.

⸻

⚙️ 6. Spread Elasticity Adjustment

You added rust scaling. Good.

Now consider:

The distribution of NBA margins is not linear.

A 2-point edge on a 3-point spread ≠
2-point edge on a 14-point spread.

Blowouts have fatter tails.
Backdoor risk increases with spread.

Edge needs nonlinear weighting.

⸻

🏥 7. Injury Impact Modeling (Real Version)

Not just “player out.”

You want:
	•	On/off net rating impact
	•	Replacement value delta
	•	Usage redistribution effects
	•	Bench depth adjustment

One star missing affects:
	•	Pace
	•	Efficiency
	•	Variance

If you’re just using binary flags, you’re underpowered.

⸻
