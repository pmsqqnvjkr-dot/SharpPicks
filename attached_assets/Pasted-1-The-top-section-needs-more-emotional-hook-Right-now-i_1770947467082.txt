1. The top section needs more emotional hook

Right now it opens:

Not all edges are created equal.

Good.
But predictable.

Make the first paragraph feel like you’re talking directly to them.

Instead of:

Not all edges are created equal.

Consider:

Most people do not lose because they lack an edge.
They lose because they treat every edge the same.

That is sharper.
More personal.
More confrontational.

It pulls the reader in.

2. Add one short personal founder line

You mention building Sharp Picks — good.

But this can be more human.

Right now:

When I built Sharp Picks, I did not want a model that fires constantly.

Better version:

When I started building Sharp Picks, I was frustrated with models that fired constantly. They looked impressive. They were not disciplined.

That adds personality and origin story.

People subscribe to founders, not models.

3. Tighten repetition slightly

You say:
	•	Larger spreads require larger edges
	•	Larger spreads require larger edges
	•	Because mathematically they demand it

Larger spreads demand larger edges. Not because it sounds conservative. Because variance demands it.
