Perfect. For NBA spread, here’s what your algorithm needs end to end, in a way you can actually build and ship.

Output

For every game you must produce:
	•	Predicted margin (Team A by X)
	•	Cover probability for the spread you are grading against
	•	Edge vs the market line
	•	Decision: pick or no pick

1) Data you must have (minimum viable)

Market
	•	Current spread (and ideally best available spread across books)
	•	Odds for spread (-110 etc.)
	•	Open line + timestamp
	•	Current line + timestamp
	•	Closing line (for tracking)

Teams
	•	Net rating (or offense and defense ratings)
	•	Pace
	•	Recent form (last 5 to 10 games, but not over weighted)
	•	Rest days
	•	Travel distance or back to back flag

Players
	•	Injury status for key players
	•	Starter availability indicator
	•	Minutes or on off impact proxy if you have it

If any of those are missing, your pick should default to no pick.

2) Model core (two clean options)

Option A: Predict margin directly
	•	Model predicts expected margin (home minus away).
	•	Then compare to spread to compute cover probability.

Option B: Predict cover probability directly
	•	Model outputs P(cover) given the spread.

Margin first is usually easier to debug and explain.

3) Convert predicted margin to cover probability

You need an error distribution. Keep it simple:
	•	Assume prediction error ~ normal with std dev around 11 to 13 points (NBA spreads are noisy).
	•	P(cover) = P(predicted_margin - spread > 0) under that distribution.

This is what turns “we think they win by 4.2” into “we think they cover at 56%”.

4) Edge calculation
	•	Convert odds to implied probability.
	•	At -110, implied is ~52.38%
	•	Edge = cover_prob - implied_prob
	•	Your publish rule:
	•	Edge >= 3.5%
	•	AND best line still available
	•	AND line movement is not flashing danger

5) Line movement logic

This is a huge differentiator. Simple rules:
	•	If the line moved against your side by 1 point or more since your last refresh, require a higher edge (example: +1.5% extra).
	•	If the line moved in your favor, that is supportive, but do not chase late steam.

You also need a hard cutoff:
	•	Do not publish within X minutes of tip if your injury feed is laggy.

6) Selection discipline

Because your product is “restraint”:
	•	Max one pick per day
	•	If no game clears the bar, publish “No pick today”
	•	If multiple games clear, choose the highest edge after applying risk filters

Risk filters to avoid traps:
	•	Avoid huge spreads (example: > 11) unless edge is exceptional
	•	Avoid uncertain injury games unless you have confirmed statuses
	•	Avoid games with missing market data

7) What to store per pick (this matters)

Create a picks table with:
	•	game_id, home, away, start_time
	•	market_spread, market_odds, sportsbook
	•	predicted_margin
	•	cover_prob
	•	implied_prob
	•	edge
	•	confidence score (if you keep it, define it as a function of edge and data quality)
	•	line_open, line_now, line_close
	•	published_at timestamp
	•	result ATS, profit_units
	•	notes fields for reasoning text

This becomes your Pro pick log and your proof.

8) “Reasoning” generation

Do not let it ramble. Always 3 bullets pulled from data:
	•	rest advantage
	•	net rating gap
	•	pace or matchup
	•	line value and why it is still playable

Same structure every time.

9) Performance tracking that actually proves it

Track both:
	•	ATS record and units
	•	CLV (closing line value) on every pick

CLV is how you win arguments with skeptics.

10) Fail safe behavior

If any of these break, you publish no pick:
	•	odds API down
	•	injuries stale
	•	predicted margin missing
	•	line missing
	•	game start time ambiguous
