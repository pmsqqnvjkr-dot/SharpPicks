To “work” in the real world (and not just look smart in a dashboard), your Sharp Picks algorithm needs a few non negotiables. This is the backbone.

1) A clear target

Pick one and measure it cleanly:
	•	Spread cover probability
	•	Moneyline win probability
	•	Total over or under probability

If you try to predict everything at once, you end up with mush.

2) A real edge calculation

Your model must output a probability, then convert it to expected value.
	•	Convert odds to implied probability (for -110, it’s ~52.38%)
	•	Your edge = model_prob - implied_prob
	•	Only publish when edge clears your bar (your spec said 3.5%+)

If you are not doing probability and EV, you are basically picking vibes.

3) Line integrity and timing

You need to know exactly what line you are grading against:
	•	The line you recommend
	•	The time you recommended it
	•	The closing line (for CLV tracking)

Without this, you cannot prove skill or separate signal from luck.

4) A disciplined selection layer

The “model” is not the product. The filter is.
You need rules like:
	•	Max 1 pick/day
	•	No pick if confidence is high but market moved against you
	•	No pick if the best available line is gone
	•	No pick if injury news makes inputs stale

This is where Sharp Picks becomes Sharp Picks.

5) Backtesting that matches reality

Most models “win” because the test is fake. You need:
	•	No future info leakage (injuries, closing lines, end of day stats)
	•	Walk forward validation (train on past, test on future)
	•	Performance tracked by season, not one big average

6) Risk and bankroll guidance

If you do not give users a stake framework, they blow themselves up even with a positive edge.
You need at least one:
	•	Flat staking (simple)
	•	Fractional Kelly (advanced)
	•	Unit system tied to confidence bands

7) A pick log with outcomes and audit trail

Every pick should store:
	•	game_id, market, side
	•	recommended odds and sportsbook
	•	model probability, edge, confidence
	•	timestamp
	•	result, profit, closing line

No log means no trust.

8) Guardrails and compliance language

To be shippable long term:
	•	“For entertainment and informational purposes”
	•	“No guaranteed outcomes”
	•	Responsible gambling language
	•	State by state disclaimers if you expand

9) Data quality defenses

You need checks for:
	•	missing player status
	•	API outages
	•	duplicated games
	•	stale lines
	•	timezone issues

One bad ingest can ruin a week and your credibility.

10) A simple explanation layer

Users do not need your features list.
They need 2 to 4 consistent reasons like:
	•	line moved in our favor
	•	matchup advantage
	•	pace and efficiency mismatch
	•	rest and travel edge

Same format every time.

⸻

If you want the shortest possible definition of “works”:

It predicts probabilities, converts them to EV, only publishes above a threshold, logs everything for audit, and survives bad data days without breaking.
