1) Model health and performance

This is about whether the engine is working and whether it is still calibrated.

What it should measure:
	•	Coverage: did we have spreads, odds, injuries, open line, closing line
	•	Pipeline health: jobs ran, data freshness, latency, error rates
	•	Prediction health: calibration, edge distribution, confidence distribution, drift
	•	Betting performance on a clean baseline:
	•	“1 unit per published pick”
	•	fixed odds assumption (or actual market odds at publish time)
	•	grading rules defined once
	•	no user inputs involved

Key rule:
Model performance is computed from published picks only, graded with your rules.
It is not “what people did with it.”

Where it shows up:
	•	Admin dashboard
	•	Optional public “transparency page” but still based on published picks, not users

2) User tracked bets

This is about the customer’s behavior and results.

What it should measure:
	•	Bets placed (pick or manual)
	•	Stake sizing (their units)
	•	Odds they actually got
	•	Their ROI, win rate, profit
	•	Optional tags: “followed Sharp Pick”, “custom bet”, “parlay”, “live bet”

Key rule:
User performance is their execution.
If they change stake, shop lines poorly, parlay, or skip days, that is on the user side, not the model side.

Where it shows up:
	•	User dashboard only
	•	Never mixed into model marketing stats

⸻

The clean split you want in product language

“Sharp Picks Performance”
	•	Your official track record
	•	Based on published picks
	•	Standardized unit sizing
	•	Auditable pick log

“Your Performance”
	•	What you actually bet
	•	Your odds, your stake, your timing
	•	Shows how closely you followed the system

⸻

How to connect them without blending them

For each user bet, store:
	•	source = sharp_pick | manual
	•	if sharp_pick:
	•	pick_id
	•	follow_type = exact | partial | late_line | parlayed
	•	odds_at_bet vs odds_at_publish
	•	line_at_bet vs line_at_publish

Then you can show:
	•	Adherence score: “You followed 7 of 10 picks exactly”
	•	Execution delta: “Your average line was 0.6 points worse than publish”
	•	Outcome split:
	•	“Model: +3.2u”
	•	“You: +1.4u”
	•	“Difference: line quality + stake variance”

That way you are honest, and it actually teaches discipline, which is your brand.

⸻

What not to do
	•	Do not let user tracked bets change the headline model ROI
	•	Do not show model health warnings to users in raw form
	•	Do not compute “accuracy” on user-entered bets because they can enter anything
