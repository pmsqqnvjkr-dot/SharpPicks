Article: Not Every Edge Is Worth Taking

Category: Discipline
Read time: 2 min
Tagline: The difference between betting and trading risk.

⸻

Not all edges are created equal.

A projected 3.6 percent edge on a 3 point spread is not the same as a 3.6 percent edge on an 11 point spread.

Variance scales with spread size. So does risk.

Most bettors treat every edge as equal. They see green and they click.

That is how volume quietly destroys ROI.

When I built Sharp Picks, I did not want a model that fires constantly. I wanted one that understands risk structure.

That’s why we use graduated thresholds. Larger spreads require larger edges. Not because it sounds conservative. Because mathematically they demand it.

If risk increases, conviction must increase.

Otherwise you are not betting with edge. You are betting with noise.

Sharp Picks is built to pass more than it plays.

Discipline is not a feature. It is the edge.

– Erin

⸻
