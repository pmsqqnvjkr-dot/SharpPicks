1) First, lock the ground truth

Before we tune thresholds, you need one clean run where you know sigma is real.

Do this after retrain:
	•	Log margin_mae and margin_std
	•	Save margin_std into your model artifact
	•	Add a banner in logs: USING_FALLBACK_SIGMA = True/False

If fallback is true, nothing else matters.

2) The threshold you actually have right now

You are effectively gating picks with:
	•	confidence >= min_confidence
	•	adjusted_edge >= EDGE_THRESHOLD_PCT
	•	Line move penalty
	•	If abs(spread) > MAX_SPREAD_DEFAULT then require adjusted_edge >= 8.0

That’s good. But it is not complete until you validate two things:
	•	Are your confidence scores calibrated
	•	Is your edge correlated with outcomes

3) What “should” EDGE_THRESHOLD be

The clean way is: pick the threshold that maximizes expected utility, not hit rate.

You should evaluate thresholds on:
	•	ROI
	•	Expected value (EV) summed
	•	Max drawdown
	•	Pick frequency (picks per week)
	•	Stability over time (month to month variance)

Run a backtest sweep:
	•	EDGE_THRESHOLD from 2.0% to 10.0% in 0.5% steps
	•	min_confidence from 0.52 to 0.60 in 0.01 steps

Pick the combo that gives you:
	•	ROI positive
	•	Drawdown tolerable
	•	Pick rate matches the brand promise (like 1 to 3 per week)

If your brand is “restraint,” then you are allowed to sacrifice volume.

4) Calibration check that matters

If your confidence is not calibrated, your edge is fake.

Do this simple test:
Bucket predictions by confidence:
	•	0.50 to 0.55
	•	0.55 to 0.60
	•	0.60 to 0.65
	•	0.65 to 0.70

For each bucket compute:
	•	Actual win rate
	•	Average implied probability
	•	Average edge
	•	Brier score

What you want:
	•	Actual win rate roughly tracks confidence
	•	Buckets above 0.57 outperform market more often

If your 0.60 bucket only wins 52%, your model is overconfident and you need to tighten or recalibrate.

5) Fix a subtle issue in edge math

You are using:
edge = (confidence - implied_prob) * 100

That’s fine, but you also should confirm your odds assumption.

I saw odds: -110 hardcoded in the frontend. If that is also used in edge, you are not using true market pricing.

Minimum requirement:
	•	Use real odds from the sportsbook feed per game
	•	Or force the product to be “spread only at -110 assumption” and say that clearly

If you can pull market odds, do it. Edge becomes real.

6) The line movement penalty should be tuned, not guessed

Right now you penalize when line moves against you by 1+ point:
line_move_penalty = line_move_against * LINE_MOVE_PENALTY_PER_PT

Pressure test it like this:
	•	Try penalty per point: 0, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0
	•	Measure ROI and pick count

If penalty is too strong, you will filter good picks.
If too weak, you chase steam and lose value.

7) Your “spread too large” rule is good, but make it coherent

This rule:
	•	big spreads need bigger edge

That is smart, but define it explicitly as a curve instead of a hard cutoff.

Example:
	•	Spread 0 to 7: threshold 3.5%
	•	Spread 7 to 11: threshold 5.0%
	•	Spread 11+: threshold 8.0%

That makes the system explainable and consistent.

8) What I would set as your default MVP gates

If you want 1 to 3 picks per week:
	•	min_confidence: 0.56
	•	EDGE_THRESHOLD_PCT: 4.0
	•	spread 11+: require 8.0
	•	line move penalty: 1.0 per point (then tune)

Then adjust based on backtest sweep.

9) The output you should publish to users

To align with your brand:
	•	“No pick today” is a feature
	•	You should publish pass reasons

Users trust a system that says:
	•	“Edge below threshold”
	•	“Line moved against us”
	•	“Spread too large for available edge”

That is how you build belief.
