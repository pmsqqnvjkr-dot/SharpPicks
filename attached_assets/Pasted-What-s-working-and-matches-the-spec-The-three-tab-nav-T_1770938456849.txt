Now — honest concerns:
1. That 33% edge is enormous and almost certainly wrong. No sports betting model produces 33% edge on a single game. The sharpest models in the world find 2-5% edges. An 85% cover probability on a -7.5 spread means your model thinks the Lakers win by 20+ points with near-certainty. If your model is routinely producing edges of 13.6% (your published average), your model is either miscalibrated or you’ve found something nobody else has. The former is far more likely.
What’s probably happening: Your cover probability calculation is too aggressive. If you’re using a points-based model (predicted margin vs spread), small errors in predicted margin get amplified when converted to probabilities. A model that predicts Lakers by 19.8 (your predicted margin) when the spread is -7.5 will show a massive edge — but that 19.8 prediction likely has a standard deviation of 10-12 points, which would bring the cover probability much closer to 65-70%, not 85%.
Fix: Make sure your cover probability accounts for prediction uncertainty. If your model predicts a margin of +19.8, you need to model the distribution around that prediction (typically normal with SD ~10-12 for NBA), then calculate P(actual margin > 7.5) from that distribution. That should give you something like 70-75%, not 85%.
2. 6-2 on 8 settled picks is meaningless sample size. You know this — your calibration section says it. But your marketing shouldn’t lean on +38.2% ROI yet. That number will regress. At 50+ settled picks you’ll have a real signal.
3. The -38.5% max drawdown is concerning for 8 picks. That means at some point you were down nearly 40% of your bankroll. With only 8 resolved picks, that’s a 2-loss streak at the wrong time. This will smooth out with volume, but it highlights the variance in small samples.
4. Philadelphia 76ers +24.5 at 47.5% edge, result: -100u. A 47.5% edge that lost is either variance (it happens) or a clear sign the edge calculation is inflated. If your model is showing 47.5% edge on any single game, something is wrong with the calibration. No market is that inefficient.
What I’d do right now:
Audit the cover probability function. It’s the core of everything. If it’s consistently overestimating by 20-30%, your edge calculations are meaningless and your pick selection is based on noise. The predicted margin numbers look reasonable (+19.8), but the probability conversion is where the bug likely lives. Fix that and the entire system gets honest.
